About Dr. Kalam

Avul Pakir Jainulabdeen Abdul Kalam, fondly known as the Missile Man of India, was born on 15th October 1931 in Rameswaram, Tamil Nadu. Despite coming from a humble background, he rose to become one of the most respected scientists and the 11th President of India.

A key figure in India's missile development programs, he played a pivotal role in the Pokhran-II nuclear tests. Beyond science, his humility, wisdom, and dedication to education made him a role model for millions, especially students across the nation.

Milestones in his Journey

Dr. Kalam served as a scientist at ISRO and DRDO, where he led many groundbreaking projects. He was awarded the Padma Bhushan, Padma Vibhushan, and the Bharat Ratna. As President (2002–2007), he was known as the “People’s President” for his genuine connection with the youth.

Post-presidency, he continued to inspire the nation through his books like *Wings of Fire*, *Ignited Minds*, and countless speeches. His life was a testament to the power of dreams, hard work, and service to the country.
